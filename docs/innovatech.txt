 School Name

 Innovatech Art & Science Academy

 Mission Statement

To cultivate a new generation of thinkers, creators, and innovators at the intersection of art, technology, and science. Our
 mission is to provide an interdisciplinary education that empowers students to solve complex challenges and contribute
 positively to society.

Target Audience

    • High school graduates interested in art, science, and technology
    • Professionals looking to upskill or transition into interdisciplinary fields
    • Lifelong learners passionate about the confluence of art, technology, and science

 Courses and Syllabus

 Course 1: Introduction to Computational Art

Topics and Subtopics

   1. Introduction to Computational Thinking
         ◦ Problem-Solving
         ◦ Algorithms
         ◦ Debugging
   2. Basics of Programming in Processing
         ◦ Setup and Draw Functions
         ◦ Variables
         ◦ Conditional Statements
   3. Generative Art
         ◦ Randomness
         ◦ Perlin Noise
         ◦ Particle Systems

 Resources and Methods

    • Textbook: "Processing: A Programming Handbook for Visual Designers and Artists"
    • Online Tutorials
    • Hands-on Projects

 Theoretical and Practical Components

    • Lectures
    • Weekly Assignments
    • Final Project: Create a piece of generative art

 Course 2: BioArt and Science

Topics and Subtopics

   1. Introduction to Biology and Art
         ◦ Cell Biology
         ◦ Bioluminescence
   2. Biological Materials in Art
         ◦ DNA
         ◦ Bacteria
         ◦ Plant Material
   3. Ethical Considerations
         ◦ Bioethics
         ◦ Environmental Impact

 Resources and Methods

    • Textbook: "Bio Art: Altered Realities"
    • Lab Experiments
    • Gallery Visits

 Theoretical and Practical Components

    • Lectures
    • Lab Sessions
    • Final Project: Create a piece of BioArt

 Course 3: Virtual Reality and Immersive Experiences

Topics and Subtopics

   1. Introduction to Virtual Reality (VR)
         ◦ History of VR
         ◦ VR Technologies
   2. Creating VR Worlds
         ◦ 3D Modeling
         ◦ Texturing and Lighting
   3. Interactivity in VR
         ◦ User Interface Design
         ◦ Interaction Design

 Resources and Methods

    • Textbook: "Understanding Virtual Reality"
    • VR Headsets for Hands-on Experience
    • Software: Unity or Unreal Engine

 Theoretical and Practical Components

    • Lectures
    • Weekly Assignments
    • Final Project: Create a simple VR experience

 These are just examples to get the curriculum started. Each course would also include guest lectures from experts in the
 field, workshops, and collaborative projects to provide a well-rounded educational experience.
